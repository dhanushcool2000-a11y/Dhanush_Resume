About Me

Most visual designers own the look. I own the system underneath it: the guidelines, templates, and visual logic that let marketing, product, and sales teams ship on-brand work without routing every asset through a designer. That's the gap I specialize in closing at fast-scaling SaaS companies.

Day to day, that means turning dense product logic into pitch decks, landing pages, and campaigns that a prospect understands in seconds. It also means building the underlying system so the next version of that asset takes hours, not days, for whoever builds it next.

At Growfin, the visual system I standardized became the default reference for every marketing and sales asset shipped. It cut down one-off design requests and kept the brand consistent as the team scaled.

Why Work With Me

I've always felt that great design doesn't happen in isolation. It comes from conversations: sharing ideas, bouncing thoughts across teams, and listening to the people who actually use or experience what we create. That's where real clarity comes from.

For me, the sweet spot is when purpose and design meet: not design for the sake of looking good, but design shaped by real insight. And the final layer is constantly thinking about how to communicate the message in the simplest, most impactful way for the audience.

Where AI Fit In

AI became part of the workflow at Growfin, but selectively, and always with a designer's judgment layered on top.

For presentations, webinars, and speaker sessions, everything stayed fully manual. Dense battlecards were the exception. I fed the underlying content to AI to generate a first-pass structure, tables, and layout scaffolding, then took over to refine it and add the polish and clarity a human eye brings. The goal was never to hand off the job. It was to offload the repetitive setup so more energy could go into the actual design thinking.

Blog banners for thought leadership and feature launches were the most automated part of the process. After months of illustrating them by hand, I trained the AI on Growfin's specific visual style and tone, then worked through each post's content with it in enough depth that it understood what the piece was actually saying. Over time, that meant less manual drawing and full visual consistency across a growing backlog of posts.

Social content stayed deliberately AI-free. Once the brand system was set, keeping an obvious human touch mattered more than speed, so templates were built and refined based on competitor research and where the product needed to push next.

Marketing collateral, including one-pagers, four-pagers, and brochures, followed the same principle. Sales and marketing needed to edit these constantly and on short notice, so I built the templates in Google Slides instead of Figma or Illustrator. That meant they could add their own content directly, export to PDF, and never touch a tool they didn't already know.
